About

PROCYCLE was heavily inspired by Spotle and Wordle.

PROCYCLE was made to help people learn something about pro cyclists they don't know. The hope is that you take what you learned and use it to become more invested and interested in the riders on your screen when you watch WorldTour bike racing. PROCYCLE is a game that not only tests your cycling knowledge but also grows it.

A big thanks to procyclingstats.com for making this game possible through the statistics on their website.

Find a bug? Have a suggestions? Let me know!

How to Play

Your Goal:

Guess the unkown pro cyclist.

Each guess reveals that cyclist's attributes and compares them to the attributes of the unkown cyclist.

Directions

1

Search for a cyclist to make your first guess.

2

Use the results to inform your next guess.

3

Piece together the clues until you guess the cyclist.

Game Mode

Daily:

A new cyclist every day. Only tens guesses and could be anyone in the list. The Daily will be different riders everyday until the end of each month, when it will then reset to the full list.

Unlimited:

You can practice as many times as you wish. any difficulty, gender, and guess setting.

Difficulty

Difficulty is based on the number and popularity of cyclists in the game.

Noob

20 of the most famous pro cyclists today.

Easy

About 200 of the most well-known cyclists.

Medium

About half of the cyclists in the game.

Hard

The entire list. Roughly 1000 cyclists.

The full breakdown of which cyclists are in the list is available under the attributes tab.

Which pro cyclists are in the game?

The complete list of cyclists includes:

● Every cyclist from all 18 mens UCI World Tour teams and all 14 womens UCI World Tour teams.

● Every cyclist from the following mens UCI ProTeams: Tudor Pro Cycling, Cofidis, Pinarello Q36.5 Pro Cycling, and Unibet Rose Rockets.

● Pro cyclists with more than 10,000 career UCI points who retired after 2020.

● A few additions on UCI ProTeams that were not included: Jesús Herrada, Fernando Gaviria, Iván Ramiro Sosa, Anthony Turgis, Allison Jackson, and Chris Froome (Not retired but not on a team).

● There are roughly 1000 cyclists in the complete list.
